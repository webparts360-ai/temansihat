BookDoc – Competitor Briefing for Telehealth, Search & Corporate Wellness

BookDoc is a regional “one‑stop health app” offering doctor search & booking, teleconsultation, and a gamified wellness programme that rewards users for being active. It operates B2C, B2B, and B2B2C models, partnering with corporates, insurers, and hospitality brands across Southeast Asia.

Visit BookDoc website ↗

From a competitor’s lens, BookDoc owns “search & access” (find providers fast) plus “move & get rewards” engagement; to beat them you must offer tighter clinical integration, deeper corporate outcomes, or a more focused vertical play.

Core Position

“ZocDoc‑style” search & booking plus telehealth and an Activ wellness rewards programme in one app.

Differentiator

Large provider network & lifestyle partners, location‑based convenience, and cross‑country reward redemptions.

Our Edge Opportunity

Out‑perform on medical depth, corporate ROI, and integrated benefits/claims, not just discovery and step rewards.

Business model and value proposition

  • Core model: multi‑sided platform connecting consumers, corporates, insurers, and healthcare providers; combines search & booking, teleconsultation, and wellness rewards.
  • Target segments: general consumers needing access to providers; corporates seeking wellness programmes; insurers and partners wanting engagement and health promotion tools.
  • Revenue drivers: corporate wellness contracts (HRDC‑claimable programmes), teleconsultation fees, potential listing/lead‑gen value to providers, and partnership revenues with lifestyle brands and insurers.
  • Value story: convenient 24/7 access to healthcare, employee wellness engagement via Activ, and marketing reach for healthcare providers and merchant partners.

Competitive insight: they monetise network effects and engagement; your edge should be in measurable health outcomes and cost control rather than just convenience and rewards.

Solutions, features, and user benefits

Consumer & patient‑facing solutions

  • Search & book: directory of healthcare professionals (doctors, specialists, dentists, physiotherapists, chiropractors, TCM, etc.) with online booking.
  • Teleconsultation: text/video consultations by selecting providers, paying a nominal fee, and chatting in‑app (non‑emergency use).
  • Integrated travel & navigation: links to map, ride‑hailing, travel booking and review platforms for travel‑related care.
  • Activ wellness rewards: step‑based and activity tracking programme that rewards users every month with discounts and cashback from many merchants across multiple countries.

Corporate and insurer solutions

  • HRDC‑claimable corporate wellness programmes with lifestyle management, health challenges, webinars and health carnivals.
  • Partnerships with insurers to power corporate wellness initiatives and employee engagement campaigns.
  • Workplace wellness engagements that combine online challenges with on‑ground events.

Benefits narrative

Consumers
  • Fast discovery of nearby providers, including public sector clinics.
  • Teleconsult access when travelling or for sensitive topics.
  • Rewards for daily activity and cross‑border redemption.
Corporates
  • Engaging corporate wellness programme with measurable participation.
  • HRDC‑claimable wellness spend.
  • Health content and webinars to support employees.
Partners
  • Healthcare providers: more patient traffic via search & booking.
  • Merchant partners: access to active, health‑conscious users for rewards.
  • Hotels/resorts: improved guest experience with quick access to nearby healthcare.

Market momentum and traction

  • Launched in Malaysia around 2015 as a doctor booking platform similar to ZocDoc.
  • Seed funding included significant investment from Brunei royalty and other investors to support regional expansion.
  • Operations in multiple Southeast Asian countries (e.g., Malaysia, Singapore, Hong Kong, Thailand, Indonesia).
  • Claims of being a large integrated healthcare platform in the region with tens of thousands of healthcare professionals.
  • Dozens of corporate, insurer, hotel and merchant partnerships for wellness and rewards programmes.

BookDoc SWOT – Competitor View

Strengths
  • Wide provider network across multiple countries and a large pool of healthcare professionals.
  • Strong brand in search & booking plus 24/7 teleconsult access.
  • Highly engaging Activ rewards programme with cross‑country merchant network.
  • Partnerships with insurers, corporates, and hospitality brands.
  • First‑mover perception as a “one‑stop health app” in Malaysia and the region.
Weaknesses
  • Focus on access and engagement rather than deep clinical outcomes or chronic disease management.
  • Corporate wellness story is more about engagement events and challenges than strict ROI or cost savings.
  • Benefits/claims integration less central than for panel‑and‑TPA‑driven players.
  • Telehealth positioned for general consults, not specialised digital therapeutics.
Opportunities (for us)
  • Own clinical depth (chronic care, mental health, digital therapeutics) where BookDoc is thinner.
  • Deliver a stronger CFO/insurer value story around claims reduction and productivity, not just engagement.
  • Integrate more tightly with HR/benefits, TPAs, and panel billing to be embedded in the financial flows.
  • Provide vertical‑specific solutions (e.g., manufacturing, logistics) that go beyond generic wellness events.
Threats (to us)
  • Strong consumer brand and app downloads give them direct access to users.
  • Network and reward partnerships make them attractive to corporates and insurers as a turnkey wellness partner.
  • Regional presence and backing from high‑profile investors enable rapid expansion.

How we win: strategy vs. BookDoc

1. Compete on outcomes, not just access ›

BookDoc wins on convenience and engagement; we should win on measurable clinical and cost outcomes (chronic risk, absenteeism, claims).

Anchor our product in evidence‑based programmes and ROI reports rather than only activity challenges.

2. Go deeper on corporate & insurer integration ›

Integrate tightly with HR systems, TPAs, and benefits platforms so interventions are linked to claims, panel usage, and costs.

Make it easy for insurers to bundle our solution as a risk‑reduction benefit, similar to but deeper than existing wellness partnerships.

3. Out‑specialise them in key verticals ›

Design sector‑tailored journeys (e.g., shift work, fatigue, ergonomics, safety) where generic Activ‑style challenges are insufficient.

Offer industry‑specific dashboards and interventions that speak directly to line managers and HSE teams.

4. Blend rewards with clinical progression ›

BookDoc’s Activ rewards steps; we can reward completion of clinically meaningful milestones (screenings, medication adherence, coaching sessions).

Use gamification to drive actual health outcomes and not just step counts.

5. Own trust and depth in care delivery ›

Invest in high‑quality telehealth networks, care teams, and protocols to deliver more complex care than general teleconsults.

Position ourselves as a long‑term health partner, not just a directory and rewards app.
